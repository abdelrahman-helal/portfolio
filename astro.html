In a couple of days, we will witness the anniversary of one of the most important Astronomy Scientists in History “Martin Shmedit”

It all started on the day of 5th of February 1963 when Martin discovered the first-ever Quasar and here is a brief introduction about what he discovered.

Firstly, Quasar is short for “Quasi Staller Radio Sources” which basically is because they’re detected by Radio Telescopes either on earth or in space.

Postscript: You can see Quasars with basic telescopes, but they will just appear as a regular star. However, there will be a huge difference between a picture on a regular telescope and a picture on a radio telescope in which we will talk about now.

Scientists call the Quasar “The Most Distant, The Brightest, and The Farthest.”

If we explain each one of those, we would require a report for each one but we will try to make it simple.

Let’s first explain how a quasar is formed; From what Astrophysicists know, most - if not all- Supermassive black holes are in the center of galaxies, and there is something called accretion disk which consists of the matter revolving around the black hole and they’re always very swift and have huge velocity when they get close to the black hole because of the huge gravitational force. During this revolution, there is an intense interaction between this matter, which generates a lot of radiation and charged particles that get attracted to the black hole’s magnetic field forming the “Quasar Jet.”

The particles inside the Quasar jet can move huge distances and in some cases, the displacement of them can be 1.5 Million Light years, larger that the galaxy in which the quasar was formed. That shows why it’s called the most distant

And now with the brightest, the brightness of one Quasar is minimally about 100 times the brightness of the milky way galaxy.

Finally, it’s called the furthest because the furthest thing we were able to see in the universe is the ULAS J1120+0641 which is a Quasar that has a distance of about 29 Billion Light years from Earth. Don’t get astonished and confused by the number, you read it correctly. Even, the closest Quasar from us is 730 Million light years away.

Scientists told us that if we could see the Quasar Exactly when it is formed after the explosion it would give us tons of information about its nature and physical properties.

Everything in the universe has laws that control it and explain its movement and properties. This is normal, but it is not normal that these laws vary entirely depending on the size of the body and its mass. this difference leads to a semi-contradictory situation between them.

After many researches and tests, the scientists agreed to make each class have its own laws that apply to it even if the equations of each class contradict the equations of a second class, but the scientists could not reconcile the use of the laws of this category because it is simply correct. You can find that objects that exist in our daily lives, such as vehicles, planes, and trains, apply Newton's equations of motion, while tiny atomic objects apply to the laws of quantum mechanics. But for giant objects like planets and stars, they’re controlled by relativity, which is the topic of this article.

Albert Einstein, the principal founder of theory of relativity, divided his research into two parts, or we could say two theories: the first theory is the theory of special relativity and the second is general relativity.

In Einstein’s special relativity, published in 1905, he proved that the speed of light is constant, which is approximately 3×10^8. This is different from the earlier, when the world considered that the speed of light is a relative velocity that changes depending on where the light is measured. Anyway, after the light was placed as a constant, Einstein was able to get to the equation E=mc2. This was based on the idea of converting mass to energy and vice versa through a process called Nuclear Fusion: the merging of the nuclei of two or more atoms. Its most known application is the hydrogen bomb.

As for the general relativity, published in 1916, it was said that time and space meet each other to form the fabric of space-time, and in relation to the concept of gravity, Einstein explained that large objects such as stars descend beneath this fabric and the rest of the objects lighter than them like planets revolve around them in the space that bends from the fabric. This subject occurs not only with the stars but also with the planets and how the moons orbit them and this explains how the planets that have a large mass in the solar system such as Jupiter and Saturn cause more moons to orbit them unlike the lighter planets.

This only an overview of the two theories, but their applications extend to describe most of what happens outside the earth’s atmosphere.

When the concept of time travel is mentioned, our minds go to one of the most complex problems that's associated with it, "Grandfather paradox."

The Grandfather paradox is a logical problem that must be avoided if a person were to time travel, it's the idea that would arise if you were to go back in time and kill your grandfather (before he could have children) which will make your own birth impossible, but won't that make you not even existing? If so, how did you actually do such action? Where are you now and what's your place in the universe?

To solve such a problem one of the solutions suggested that you going back in time actually makes an alternative universe with different events where you didn't exist and your grandfather is dead, as we saw in" X-men days of the future past" going back in time and recreating events make a whole new universe as we said before and such thing was also mentioned in "Avengers: end game " However, Let's say there are no alternative universes, no such simple solutions, what would really happen?

This loop of events is actually two entangled histories happening in parallel. To understand this phenomenon, we first need to know about "Quantum superposition." It states a physical system may be in one of many configurations—arrangements of particles or fields—then the most general state is a combination of all of these possibilities and so.

If the universe did exist in a superposition of two states (your grandfather is alive and your grandfather is dead) then the natural result is a superposition of two states (You are born and able to go back in time and kill your grandfather and you are not born) So somehow this loop is not consistent, and there is really a paradox.

However, we must remember that time loops, according to our current knowledge and technology, are nearly impossible. So, the only paradox here is how our thinking can be twisted enough to dream about time travelling and killing our grandfathers!!

About 4.5 billion years ago, in the dark space in one of the milky way arms, lied a cloud of gas and dust that started to collapse on itself because of gravity; the particles were fighting against this pressure, and the temperature rose to some million Fahrenheits. When the temperature was high enough, the hydrogen gas gained the power to undergo nuclear reactions and announce the birth of our closest star, the sun.

Once the sun started nuclear reactions, it entered a phase called Main Sequence, which is the main stage in the life of a star. In this stage, the star generates energy from nuclear fusion. Simply, it merges four hydrogen atoms into one helium atom. Since the mass of the helium atom is less than the combined mass of the four hydrogen atoms, this mass defect is converted to energy in the form of neutrinos and gamma rays. The calculations state that the sun fuses almost 600 million tons of hydrogen each second that result in the generation of 3.84 × 10^26 joule of energy.

This process will continue for 10 billion years ( 4.5 of them passed already) until most of the hydrogen in the core is converted into helium. When this occurs, the sun will no longer be a main sequence star. It becomes a Red Giant. It will merge the helium into beryllium atoms, its radius will be nearly 100 times the current radius, and the surface temperature will decrease.

When the fusion stops, there won’t be any energy or pressure balancing the tremendous gravity of the sun. So, the sun’s mass will collapse on itself until it reaches a point in which electrons of the core’s atoms can’t be compressed anymore. At this moment, the sun becomes a White Dwarf, with very high density, high surface temperature, small radius, and rapid rotation. Finally, it will continue to shine for some years until it cools and becomes a dark body in the space.
